Ripple Effect: Water Conservation in Student Housing

Water in Amsterdam is low in cost but limited in supply. This resource could run out in the coming decade. Scroll down to learn about water scarcity in the Netherlands and understand how you can use less.

Our Dilemma

The problem with droughts in The Netherlands


The Netherlands is in another year of drought, caused primarily by a lack of reliable reservoirs to store water.

Droughts increase flood risks and prevent water flows. Amsterdam also has many old homes that are at risk of sinking or collapsing due to prolonged drought.


Amsterdam's rising population burdens water infrastructure.

To avoid high costs falling on tax payers to redo the infrastructure, citizens can make small habit changes now to provide more water for everyone. The goal is for every person to use a maximum of 130 liters/day.

Your Data

Water Usage in Amsterdam



Students generally fall within the 16 - 25 year old category. This age group has the highest average daily water usage at 196 liters/day.


Daily habits to get below 130 liters per day:

  • Cut the shower short by 3 minutes. This saves 22 liters.
  • Use the partial flush. This can save 15 liters.
  • Skip one load of laundry per month. This saves nearly 50 liters.
  • Take one less shower per week. For an 8 minute shower, this saves around 60 liters.
  • Switch to a water-saving shower head. This saves 20 liters per shower.

    Most students pay a flat fee for water use. This is typically incorporated into "service costs" in the rental contract. However, almost every student housing agency charges for excess water consumed past the expected average. Avoid additional fees and reduce your consumption.     Our Tips

    Ways to Save Water

    Shower

    There are two simple ways to save most effectively in the shower....
    Take a shorter shower and use a softer setting. Start timing your showers and see how often you can shave a minute or two off. A typical shower uses 7.8 liters per minute. If possible, switch to a water-saving showerhead.

    Dishes

    It is commonly believed that dishwashers are more water-efficient....
    But, doing the dishes by hand uses half the volume of water. 5 liters from handwashing and nearly 12 liters by machine. Keep the tap on a low setting or turn it off while scrubbing.
    If you have a dishwasher, only run it when full and skip pre-rinsing the dishes.

    Laundry

    Saving water on laundry can be very simple....
    Wash below 30 degrees and make sure the load is full.
    Modern detergents prevent colors from blending, meaning separate loads are not necessary.
    Be efficient by only doing one or two washes. Re-wear clothes beyond a single use to prevent unneeded cycles. Skipping one load saves nearly 50 liters of water.

    Cooking

    Cooking doesn't always need so much water....
    Instead of running the tap while rinsing produce, fill one bowl to use for washing. Be mindful about filling pots to boil, using a lid to conserve as the water heats. You can also save excess water to repurpose for the garden or to flush the toilet.

    Outside

    You may care for plant babies, whether they live inside or outside....
    Roughly 4.6 liters are used each time for watering. Reuse excess cooking or shower water, or capture rain water to tend to plants.

    Toilet

    The toilet should only be flushed for certain reasons....
    And trash, such as tissues, is not one of them. Only flush bodily waste, and use the partial flush option (the smaller button) if possible. On average, people flush 3 - 4 times a day. A full flush uses 6.7 liters, while a partial flush only requires 3.7 liters.
